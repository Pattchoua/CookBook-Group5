Currywurst

Description

The birth of the currywurst took place only a few years after the second World War. According to the Welt der Wunder, the snack stand owner Herta Heuwer reported that it was a rainy day when she mixed the first curry sauce on September 4th, 1949, clearly out of boredom. She wanted to spice up her meal, so she traded some German alcoholic beverages to the English for some curry powder, The ingredients were reputed to be peppers, ground pepper, tomato puree, and different spices – she then poured the signature creation over a boiled and cut into pieces sausage.

Ingredients

Currywurst
8 German brats (alternative stadium brats or jumbo hot dogs) 2 tbsp. honey
1 tbsp. olive oil 1 tsp. soy sauce
1 small red onion finely cubed 2 tbsp. curry powder
16 oz. ketchup ¼ tsp. chili powder or cayenne pepper
1 tbsp. tomato paste 1-2 dash hot sauce (optional)
2 tbsp. water 1 tsp. white vinegar (optional)
2 tbsp. dark balsamic vinegar

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. In the meantime, cube the onion finely.
  2. Step 2: Add onion cubes to the saucepan and fry until translucent.
  3. Step 3: Add tomato paste and roast with the onions for a couple of minutes on medium-high heat.
  4. Step 4: Deglaze with about 2 tbsp. of water.
  5. Step 5: Add ketchup, balsamic glaze and honey and cook over medium-low heat for a few minutes.
  6. Step 6: Stir in soy sauce, chili powder and curry powder.
  7. Step 7: Transfer to a tall blender bowl and blend with a hand blender to a smooth texture. Alternative you can blend it in a stand mixer or a single serve blender.
  8. Step 8: Taste test and add about 1 tsp. to ½ tbsp. white vinegar if you prefer a tangy flavor. Add a dash or two of hot sauce if you like it spicy!
  9. Step 9: The sauce should have a thick, honey like consistency. Add water if necessary or if you prefer a more liquid consistency.
  10. Step 10: Keep the sauce warm, in the meantime grill the sausages.
  11. Step 11: Ladle the warm sauce over the sausages and sprinkle with additional curry powder to taste.
