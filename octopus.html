Sweet Peas with Octopus

Description

This dish embodies the essence of Greek cuisine, showcasing the harmonious combination of seafood and legumes. With every bite, you’ll be transported to the country’s picturesque coastal villages, where the flavors of the sea mingle with the freshness of the land. So, indulge in this culinary delight and let the flavors of arakas laderos transport you to the azure waters of the Mediterranean. Enjoy the full recipe below

Ingredients

for the Octopus for the peas
1.5 kg octopus 1 kg sweet peas
100 ml Greek extra virgin olive oil 1 small onion finely chopped
1 large onion thinly sliced 100 g carrots finely chopped
1 large carrot cut into thin vertical slice 800 g celery finely chopped
1 fennel cut into thin vertical slices 1 tbsp tomato pastes
1 tbsp balsamic vinegar 100 ml red wine
½ tbsp peppercorns 400 g grated tomato
2 bay leaves 100 ml water
6 sprigs thyme salt and pepper/td>

Instructions

    .
  1. Step 1: At the bottom of a wide pot, spread the onion, carrot and fennel. Add the balsamic vinegar, peppercorns, bay leaves, thyme and set aside.
  2. Step 2: Pierce the circumference of the octopus tooth with a straight knife and remove it.
  3. Step 3: Cut the head of the octopus and cut it in four pieces. Divide the tentacles and place them over the vegetables
  4. Step 4: Sprinkle with a little olive oil, cover the pot with aluminum foil and simmer the octopus over low heat for 1 1/2 hour, until it softens. When ready, remove from heat and set aside.
  5. Step 5: While the octopus is being cooked, we can start cooking the peas as well. Place a large pot over medium to high heat and let it heat well. Then add a little olive oil, onion, carrot, celery and sauté until caramelized
  6. Step 6: Once the vegetables are caramelized, add the tomato paste, sauté for 2-3 minutes and add wine. Let the alcohol evaporate, add the peas, tomato, water, salt and pepper and mix.
  7. Step 7: Cover the pot with the lid and let the food boil for 20-25 minutes.
  8. Step 8: After 25 minutes, transfer to a baking dish, add the octopus and cook in a preheated oven at 180°C (360 °F) for 10 to 15 minutes.
  9. Step 9: When ready, serve the peas with octopus, sprinkling with some dill.
